Abstract

In recent years, the proliferation of deepfake technology has raised significant concerns regarding misinformation and digital security. This project aims to develop a robust machine learning-based system for detecting deepfake videos and images. We utilize a combination of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to analyze both spatial and temporal features of multimedia content.
Our approach begins with a comprehensive dataset of authentic and manipulated media. We preprocess the data to enhance feature extraction and then train our model on this dataset. The system evaluates visual artifacts, inconsistencies in facial expressions, and subtle anomalies that typically characterize deepfakes.
We assess the model's performance using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score. Initial results demonstrate promising detection rates, indicating that our method can effectively distinguish between genuine and altered media. Ultimately, this project aims to contribute to the development of tools that help mitigate the impact of deepfakes, fostering a more trustworthy digital environment.

Introduction

In the digital age, the rise of artificial intelligence has led to the creation of highly realistic manipulated media known as deepfakes. These synthetic videos and images use advanced algorithms to alter appearances and voices, making it increasingly challenging to discern fact from fiction. While deepfake technology has legitimate applications in entertainment and education, it also poses significant risks, including misinformation, identity theft, and potential political manipulation.
As the prevalence of deepfakes grows, so does the need for effective detection methods. Traditional techniques for identifying manipulated media are often inadequate, given the rapid advancements in deepfake creation. This project focuses on leveraging machine learning to develop a reliable detection system that can identify deepfake content with high accuracy.
